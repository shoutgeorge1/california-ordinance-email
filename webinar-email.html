Newly Signed California Laws for Property Owners and Managers

Key Legislative Updates

Modernized Contract Cancellations:

AB 1327

Allows homeowners to cancel home improvement or solicitation contracts via email or phone, simplifying the process.

Tenant Protections and Property Management Enhancements:

AB 628

Starting in 2026, rental units must include a working stove and refrigerator to meet habitability standards.

AB 246

Protects tenants from eviction if Social Security benefit delays or reductions impact rent payments.

AB 414

Permits electronic return of security deposits with tenant consent, streamlining refund processes.

AB 391

Allows mobilehome park notices to be delivered electronically with resident consent, improving communication efficiency.

AB 806

Prohibits mobilehome parks from restricting residents' installation or use of air conditioning, enhancing tenant comfort.

AB 863

Directs the creation of a multilingual eviction summons form to improve language accessibility for tenants.

Compliance and Transparency: These laws promote fair practices, helping you maintain compliance and foster positive tenant relationships.

Important Reminders:

SB 326 (Condominiums)

The deadline for initial balcony and exterior elevated element inspections was January 1, 2025. If you have not yet completed these inspections for your condominium properties, please take immediate action to ensure compliance with structural safety requirements. Contact a licensed structural engineer or inspector to address any outstanding obligations.

Action required

SB 721 (Apartment Buildings)

The deadline for balcony and exterior elevated element inspections for apartment buildings is January 1, 2026. We strongly recommend scheduling these inspections early to ensure the safety of your tenants and compliance with state law. Consult a qualified professional to plan and complete these inspections.
